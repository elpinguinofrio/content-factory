## 1. Monetization Reality

**If AdSense is registered in Russia:** YouTube monetization suspended since March 2022. No new YPP sign-ups. No memberships, Super Chat, merch shelf, or ad revenue from Russian viewers.

**If outside Russia (diaspora):** Can monetize. Ad revenue applies only to views from outside Russia. Russian viewers generate zero ad revenue.
- CPM by viewer country: Germany ~$5.50, US ~$10+, Eastern Europe $1-3, Ukraine/Kazakhstan low single digits
- YPP threshold: 1,000 subscribers AND either 4,000 watch hours OR 10M Shorts views in 90 days

**Shorts monetization math is brutal:** Shorts RPM is $0.01-$0.30 per 1,000 views in non-English markets. Long-form yields $1-25+ per 1,000 views. Russian vc.ru community: "Шортс на 100к просмотров = $5. Длинное видео на 100к = $500+". **Shorts are a growth engine, not an income engine.**

**Alternative monetization (no YPP needed):**
- Boosty.to (Russian Patreon equivalent, works at any subscriber count)
- Telegram channel with paid subscription tier
- Affiliate marketing with Russian-language product links
- Direct sponsorships (works even at small scale if audience is targeted)

**Recommendation:** At 350 subs, don't optimize for ad revenue. Optimize for reaching 1,000 subs and building community via Boosty/Telegram. Even 2% conversion at 350 subs = 7 supporters at $2-5/mo = covers tool costs.

## 2. What Matters at 350 Subs

### Do This

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Lock one micro-niche, stay 6 months | Algorithm learns your audience; broad topics reset this |
| 1 long-form/week + 2-3 Shorts/week | Consistency beats frequency; weekly is the sweet spot |
| Reply to every single comment | Builds loyalty, increases comment velocity signal |
| Track CTR + Average View Duration | These metrics determine if YouTube expands your audience |
| Open Boosty.to now | Zero minimum audience requirement |
| Create a Telegram channel | Your hub for community building |
| Karaoke-style burned-in captions | +12-25% completion rate |
| Evergreen problem-solving content | Compounds over time |

### Don't Waste Time On

| Time Sink | Why It Doesn't Help |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Upload time optimization | Negligible effect vs content quality |
| Community posts | Almost nobody sees them under 500 subs |
| Collab outreach to large channels | They won't respond; match your tier |
| Buying subscribers/views | Destroys engagement ratio; algorithm penalizes |
| Cross-posting to 5+ platforms | Dilutes energy; do 1-2 well |
| End screens optimization | Only matters with viewership volume |
| Daily Shorts without long-form library | No funnel destination; random short-lived spikes |
| Complex SEO keyword research | At 350 subs, traffic comes from Browse/Recommendations, not Search |
| Elaborate thumbnail A/B testing | Sample sizes too small to be statistically significant |
| News/current events content | Short shelf life; no compounding returns |

### Metrics That Actually Matter

- **Average View Duration (AVD)** and **Average Percentage Viewed (APV)** — determine whether YouTube expands audience
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** on thumbnails — target 4-8%
- **Subscriber-to-view ratio** on long-form — 2%+ means content is working
- Ignore total view count and total subscriber count as primary KPIs

### Phased Approach (from Russian creator community, vc.ru)

- Months 1-6: One long-form video per week only. Build format and foundational audience.
- Months 6-12: Add one Short per week extracted from long-form.
- Month 12+: Scale Shorts to higher frequency while keeping long-form as primary.

## 3. Russian-Specific Insights

### Hook Style (from Gemini)

Skip "Hey guys!" energy. Russian audiences find American-style enthusiasm "cringe" or "fake." Use direct, slightly cynical hooks:

| Hook Type | Russian Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| Provocative question | "Вы делаете эту ошибку каждый день?" | Creates curiosity gap |
| Counterintuitive | "Больше подписчиков = меньше денег" | Challenges assumption |
| Specific number | "3 ошибки за первые 30 дней" | Concrete, finite, low commitment |
| Cynical/direct | "Нас обманывали" (We were lied to) | Matches Russian communication style |
| Mid-action open | Start already doing the thing | No warm-up = no swipe |

### Caption Style for Cyrillic

- Russian words are 15-20% longer than English
- Use **bold, centered, single-word animated (karaoke-style) captions** that change quickly
- Max 2 short lines on screen at once
- High-contrast: white on black shadow, or yellow on dark
- Karaoke-style outperforms static captions by ~20% retention

### Music Trends in Russian YouTube

- **Phonk** — very popular in RU market
- **Minimalist lo-fi** — works well
- **Avoid** "happy/uplifting" corporate music common in Western vlogs

### Cultural Communication Style

- Authenticity and directness outperform polished marketing aesthetics
- "Простые и эмоциональные сюжеты" (simple, emotionally-driven narratives) work best
- Humor and relatability pull well
- "Challenge/reaction/betray expectation" format works universally

### Platform Reality (Gemini's Key Insight)

YouTube is throttled in Russia — Shorts load faster than long-form on throttled connections, making Shorts your **only reliable discovery tool** for viewers inside Russia. Mirror your best 2 Shorts/week to **VK Clips** — you're leaving ~70% of Russian audience on the table otherwise.

## 4. YouTube Algorithm for Russian Content

### Distribution

YouTube distributes Russian-language content primarily within the Russian-language viewer pool globally. Set channel language to Russian in Studio. Add Russian keywords in titles, descriptions, and tags.

### Algorithm Phases for New Content

1. **Explore phase**: Shows to ~500-2,000 test viewers. If CTR + retention strong → expands.
2. **Expansion phase**: Broader audience. Completion rate is primary signal.
3. **Recommendation phase**: Appears in "up next" and homepage for relevant viewers.

At 350 subs, most videos live in phase 1. Goal: pass phase 1 test repeatedly until algorithm has strong audience model.

### Shorts-Specific Algorithm (2025+)

- As of March 31, 2025: any playback of any duration counts as a view
- Each replay/loop counts as additional view
- **Watch-per-Swipe (WPS)** and **Average Percentage Viewed** are primary signals
- Shorts help YouTube "learn" your audience and apply that to long-form recommendations
- **% completion matters more than total watch time** — 20s Short at 90% beats 2-min Short at 30%

### What Algorithm Does NOT Care About at 350 Subs

- Total subscriber count (doesn't affect distribution)
- Like/dislike ratio (minimal signal weight)
- Comment count (small signal; replies help more than count)
- Publishing time of day (minor; consistency matters more)

### July 2025 Algorithm Change

YouTube penalizes "cloned" or derivative content with no original value. Mass-produced AI-generated videos without authentic voice get suppressed. Good news for genuine solo creators.

## 5. Content Pipeline

### One Video → Multiple Assets

```
LONG-FORM VIDEO (20-30 min)
    │
    ├── SHORT #1: Best standalone moment (15-45 sec)
    ├── SHORT #2: Provocative clip or "part 2" teaser
    ├── SHORT #3: How-to or tip extracted
    │
    ├── Telegram post: Key takeaways as text
    └── VK Clip: Best-performing Short mirrored
```

### Pre-Production: Design for Repurposing

- Script "clipable moments" — segment long-form into discrete tips/stories that stand alone
- Insert natural mini-hooks every few minutes
- Plan content calendar 6-8 weeks ahead

### Post-Production Pipeline

1. Upload long-form to AI clipping tool (Vizard, OpusClip, etc.)
2. AI surfaces 10-20 candidate clips with virality scores
3. Human review: reject clips that don't make sense without context
4. Batch-edit: captions, framing, branding, hook tightening
5. Schedule across platforms
6. Analyze performance → feed learnings into next long-form script

### Realistic Weekly Schedule (Solo Creator)

| Day | Task | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| Mon | Idea selection + outline | 45 min |
| Tue | Script draft | 60 min |
| Wed | Record long-form + 1-2 dedicated Shorts | 90 min batch |
| Thu | Edit long-form | 2-3 hr |
| Fri | Run through AI clipper → select best → fix captions | 45 min |
| Sat | Upload + descriptions + thumbnails | 45 min |
| Sun | Reply to comments + check analytics | 30 min |

**Total: ~8-10 hours/week.** Sustainable solo.

**Batch filming tip:** Film 2-3 long-form videos in one session every 2-3 weeks. Script all in one sitting, film all in one block, edit across the week.

## 6. YouTube Shorts Best Practices

### Length

- YouTube allows Shorts up to **3 minutes** (expanded 2025)
- Optimal sweet spot: **15-45 seconds** for maximum retention
- Under 20 seconds: 42% watch fully (easiest high completion)
- 15-30 seconds: highest average retention, often >80%
- Only go longer if content genuinely sustains attention

### Hook (First 3 Seconds)

- 50-60% of viewers drop off in first 3 seconds
- Hook = visual + on-screen text + opening line, all working together
- Start with payoff or conflict, not preamble
- Pattern-interrupt openers outperform setup-then-punchline

### Format

- 9:16 vertical, 1080x1920px
- Center subjects in frame
- Minimal clutter

### Upload Strategy

- Shorts: 3-7/week, spread out (never mass-dump same day)
- Space clips from same source video 3-5 days apart
- Best posting windows: 12pm-3pm and 7pm-10pm (audience timezone)
- Always include `#Shorts` + 3-5 niche hashtags
- Description CTA: link to full long-form video (funnel mechanism)

### Titles

- 55-60 chars max, front-load value proposition
- Pattern: "[Specific Result] in [Timeframe]" or "[Surprising Claim]"
- Use YouTube's A/B title testing feature

### Critical Rules

- **Never post TikTok-watermarked content** — YouTube suppresses it
- **Do NOT make separate Shorts channel** — lose cross-pollination benefit
- Shorts subscriber conversion averages only 0.12-0.18% (12-18 per 10K views)

## 9. Technical Automation Pipeline

### Whisper for Russian (Local on Apple Silicon Mac)

**Setup:**
```bash
brew install ffmpeg
python3 -m venv ~/content-factory
source ~/content-factory/bin/activate
pip install -U torch openai-whisper
```

**Run transcription:**
```bash
whisper video.mp4 \
  --model medium \
  --language Russian \
  --task transcribe \
  --output_format srt \
  --fp16 False
```

**Model choice for Russian:**
- `small`: minimum usable
- `medium`: best price/performance for solo creator (recommended)
- `large`/`turbo`: better quality, slower/heavier

**Russian WER:**
- Clean speech: ~8-12%
- Real YouTube audio: ~12-25%
- Whisper large-v3 on Common Voice Russian: ~9.84% WER

### FFmpeg Commands

**Extract audio (for ASR):**
```bash
ffmpeg -y -i input.mp4 -vn -ac 1 -ar 16000 -c:a pcm_s16le audio_16k.wav
```

**16:9 → 9:16 vertical center crop:**
```bash
ffmpeg -y -i input.mp4 \
  -vf "crop=ih*9/16:ih:(iw-ih*9/16)/2:0,scale=1080:1920:flags=lanczos,setsar=1" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset slow -crf 18 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 128k -movflags +faststart \
  vertical.mp4
```

**Burn Russian captions from SRT:**
```bash
ffmpeg -y -i vertical.mp4 \
  -vf "subtitles='captions_ru.srt':charenc=UTF-8:force_style='FontName=Arial,FontSize=54,PrimaryColour=&H00FFFFFF,OutlineColour=&H00000000,BorderStyle=1,Outline=3,Shadow=0,MarginV=120'" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset medium -crf 18 -c:a copy \
  vertical_subs.mp4
```

**Add branding watermark:**
```bash
ffmpeg -y -i vertical_subs.mp4 -i logo.png \
  -filter_complex "[1:v]scale=180:-1[wm];[0:v][wm]overlay=W-w-36:H-h-36:format=auto" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset medium -crf 18 -c:a copy \
  vertical_subs_brand.mp4
```

### Complete MVP Script (`make_short.sh`)

```bash
#!/usr/bin/env bash
set -euo pipefail

IN="${1:?Usage: $0 input.mp4 START DURATION}"
START="${2:-00:00:00}"
DUR="${3:-59}"

WORK="work_short"
mkdir -p "$WORK"

# 1) Extract clip audio
ffmpeg -y -ss "$START" -t "$DUR" -i "$IN" \
  -vn -ac 1 -ar 16000 -c:a pcm_s16le "$WORK/audio.wav"

# 2) Whisper → SRT (Russian)
whisper "$WORK/audio.wav" \
  --model medium \
  --language Russian \
  --task transcribe \
  --output_dir "$WORK" \
  --output_format srt \
  --fp16 False

# 3) Vertical crop + burn captions
ffmpeg -y -ss "$START" -t "$DUR" -i "$IN" \
  -vf "crop=ih*9/16:ih:(iw-ih*9/16)/2:0,\
scale=1080:1920:flags=lanczos,\
subtitles='$WORK/audio.srt':charenc=UTF-8:\
force_style='FontName=Arial,FontSize=54,\
PrimaryColour=&H00FFFFFF,OutlineColour=&H00000000,\
BorderStyle=1,Outline=3,Shadow=0,MarginV=120'" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset medium -crf 19 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 128k -movflags +faststart \
  "short_ready.mp4"

echo "Done → short_ready.mp4"
```

**Usage:**
```bash
chmod +x make_short.sh
./make_short.sh my_video.mp4 00:05:30 45
```

### YouTube Data API Upload Script

```python
#!/usr/bin/env python3
import argparse
from google_auth_oauthlib.flow import InstalledAppFlow
from googleapiclient.discovery import build
from googleapiclient.http import MediaFileUpload

SCOPES = ["https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube.upload"]

def main():
    p = argparse.ArgumentParser()
    p.add_argument("--file", required=True)
    p.add_argument("--title", required=True)
    p.add_argument("--description", required=True)
    p.add_argument("--publish-at", required=True, help="RFC3339, e.g. 2026-03-10T18:00:00Z")
    args = p.parse_args()

    flow = InstalledAppFlow.from_client_secrets_file("client_secret.json", SCOPES)
    creds = flow.run_local_server(port=0)
    youtube = build("youtube", "v3", credentials=creds)

    body = {
        "snippet": {
            "title": args.title,
            "description": f"{args.description}\n\n#Shorts",
            "categoryId": "22"
        },
        "status": {
            "privacyStatus": "private",
            "publishAt": args.publish_at
        }
    }

    req = youtube.videos().insert(
        part="snippet,status",
        body=body,
        media_body=MediaFileUpload(args.file, resumable=True, chunksize=-1)
    )
    resp = req.execute()
    print("Uploaded video ID:", resp["id"])

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
```

**Install:** `pip install google-api-python-client google-auth-oauthlib google-auth-httplib2`

**Quota:** 10,000 units/day. `videos.insert` = 100 units (as of Dec 4, 2025). ~100 uploads/day theoretical max.

## 11. Common Traps

| Trap | Why It Fails | Fix |
|------|-------------|-----|
| "MrBeast" clone energy | Russian audiences find hyper-expressive American enthusiasm "cringe" | Be direct, deadpan, authentic |
| Ignoring VK Video | 70% of Russian audience left on the table | Mirror best 2 Shorts/week to VK Clips |
| AdSense obsession | No AdSense in Russia | Build Boosty/consulting funnel instead |
| Random chopping without standalone value | Clips confuse new viewers | Only clip segments with clear beginning, point, end |
| TikTok watermarks on YouTube | YouTube suppresses watermarked competitor content | Export clean from source |
| Mass-posting all clips same day | Algorithm treats as spam | Space 3-5 days apart |
| Separate Shorts channel | Loses Shorts → long-form funnel benefit | One channel for everything |
| No captions | 40-50% watch muted | Burn-in captions center-screen |
| No CTA in descriptions | Wastes discovery traffic | Always link to full video |
| Chasing off-niche trends | Attracts wrong audience; tanks subscription rate | Only clip niche-aligned moments |
| Posting Shorts daily before long-form library | No funnel destination | Build long-form library first |
